Setup & notation

We consider the series \( \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n^2} \) and its relation to the Riemann zeta function \( \zeta(s) = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} n^{-s} \) for \( \Re(s) > 1 \). Throughout, \( \pi \) denotes the circle constant and \( T_n = \frac{n(n+1)}{2} \) the \(n\)-th triangular number.

Basel series

The Basel problem asks for the exact value of \( \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n^2} \). The well-known result is \( \zeta(2) = \frac{\pi^2}{6} \). We record comparison bounds and the convergence rate as \( N \to \infty \) for partial sums \( S_N = \sum_{n=1}^{N} \frac{1}{n^2} \).

Euler’s strategy

Euler’s classical approach factors \( \sin(\pi x) \) via its zeros \( \{ \pm n \}_{n\in\mathbb{N}} \) and compares coefficients of its power series with its product expansion, culminating in \( \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n^2} = \frac{\pi^2}{6} \). Variants consider \( \frac{\sin(\pi x)}{\pi x} \) and logarithmic differentiation.

Geometric view

In this notebook we track a geometric lens: how inverse-square decay relates to grid-based constructions and triangular counts. Where useful we reference figures by label (see the Figures section).

Consequences

Among immediate corollaries are relations between \( \zeta(2) \), series of chord lengths, and scaling laws used in later chapters. We also note identities for even arguments \( \zeta(2k) \).
